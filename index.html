OVERVIEW

The GW UX/UI Bootcamp is a crash course user-centric design research, design thinking, visual prototyping and wireframing, interface design, storyboarding, visual design theory, web prototyping with HTML5 and CSS, interaction design with JavaScript and jQuery, and more.

As my first introduction to UX design thinking, I was asked to understand the pain points of

how people plan to travel in the future, post-pandemic.

THE PROBLEM

millennials have difficulty budgeting & coordinating their travel plans.

Vacationing becomes anxiety inducing when planning and budgeting come into play. Whether it is solo or with a group, overspending is all too easy and planning activities is like herding cats.

THE SOLTUION

sonder is an app that curates budgets based on average area spending.

My goal was to create an app that tracks budgets from a macro perspective as oppose to a micro. Thus, providing an overall goal for the user to stick to while on vacation and less likely to overspend

USER RESEARCH

millenials

are planning the to take the most trips out of any generation: an average of 4.4 trips over the next 12 months.

61% of travelers are most

interested in outdoor activities (hiking, biking, and kayaking) and wellness/relaxation experiences.

Of travelers who subscribe to travel related paid subscriptions,

62% are male and 38% are female.

to better understand the needs of men and women ages 27-38 who travel at least 3x a year, I conducted 1x1 structured interviews with 5 participants.

It quickly became apparent that the majority of participants were concerned with their spending habits, coordinating activities and the pitfalls of the traveling process (missing flights and losing luggage).
